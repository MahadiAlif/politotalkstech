The aerospace industry is under increasing pressure to reduce its environmental footprint while maintaining the highest safety and performance standards. This literature review synthesizes current research on sustainable materials for aerospace applications.

## Bio-Based Composites

Recent advances in bio-based composite materials show promise for aerospace applications:

- Natural fiber reinforcements as alternatives to glass fiber
- Bio-derived matrix materials with comparable performance to petroleum-based resins
- Hybrid approaches combining natural and synthetic materials
- Performance characteristics suitable for non-critical structural components

## Recycling and End-of-Life Strategies

The review examines various approaches to aerospace material recycling:

- Chemical recycling methods for composite materials
- Mechanical recycling and downcycling strategies
- Design for disassembly principles
- Economic viability of recycling programs

## Life-Cycle Assessment

Comprehensive environmental impact analysis reveals:

- Total carbon footprint from raw material extraction to disposal
- Energy consumption during manufacturing and processing
- Trade-offs between weight savings and production impact
- Long-term sustainability metrics

## Challenges and Barriers

Several challenges impede widespread adoption:

- Certification requirements and safety standards
- Performance gaps in extreme conditions
- Cost competitiveness with traditional materials
- Supply chain development for bio-based materials

## Industry Perspectives

Leading aerospace manufacturers are investing in sustainable materials research, with several demonstration projects showing promising results.

## Future Directions

The review identifies key areas for future research including improved material properties, scalable manufacturing processes, and comprehensive certification frameworks.

## Conclusions

While significant challenges remain, sustainable materials represent a viable path toward reducing the aerospace industry's environmental impact without compromising safety or performance.
